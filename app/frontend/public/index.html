How to read this map

The layer is the Sentinel-5P tropospheric NO₂ column anomaly — the selected day's column minus the pixelwise historical median for the same calendar month over the previous three years. Sentinel-5P measures a vertical column through the troposphere, not the air at ground level: nothing on this map is an AQI value, health advice, or an episode classification. The 0.01° display grid is oversampled relative to the TROPOMI sensor footprint, so neighbouring display cells are not independent 1 km observations. Colours use a per-date display stretch — not a threshold — and colour intensity is not directly comparable across dates.

What this prototype does not yet assess

This application does not yet evaluate persistence across days, spatial-extent criteria, ground-monitor corroboration, or meteorological context, and it performs no episode classification. It reports one date's satellite-column evidence — with its coverage and historical context — and stops there.

Known limits of this measurement

Column, not ground level

The instrument measures NO₂ through the depth of the troposphere. That is a different physical quantity from what a monitor at street level records.

Grid is finer than the footprint

The 0.01° product grid is oversampled relative to the native footprint of roughly 3.5 × 5.5 km. Neighbouring cells are not independent kilometre-scale measurements.

Coverage varies by day

Cloud and retrieval quality decide how much of the region is seen. Identical means from 90% and 15% coverage describe very different amounts of evidence, so coverage travels with every number.

The record is not uniform

Processor and algorithm versions change through the archive. This is a rolling comparison against recent same-month history, not a homogeneous long-term trend.

Colour intensity is per-date

The anomaly map uses a per-date robust display stretch. It is not a threshold, and colour intensity is not directly comparable between dates.

Spatial coincidence is not cause

A pattern that lines up with a highway or a facility is geography, not attribution. This product cannot assign column values to a road segment or a site.

No surface validation yet

Comparison against BAAQMD and EPA surface monitors, overpass timing, and meteorological context remains future work.
